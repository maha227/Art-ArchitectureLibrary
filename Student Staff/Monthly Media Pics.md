April 2026: 

“Manuel Cervantes Céspedes / CC Arquitectos - 2018/2026: [El Clasicista Accidental =The Accidental Classicist].” 2026. Croquis, no. 223: 56-66. EBSCOhost. 

“Cardo Santo is not a visual statement piece. Its gesture is subtle and its presence measured. It is a space that allows architecture to dissolve into the environment, prioritizing the experience of living over form. It is a place where time seems to stop, not because of grandiosity, but because of precision.” 

Casa Club Cardo Santo is an example of architecture that works with the environment and builds from it instead of working against it. As the quote suggests, the piece is eye-catching and interesting because of how well it flows with its surroundings, through the materials used, the openness of the structure, and how it was placed in the site. Manuel Cervantes created a structure that followed the topography of the land, emphasized sunlight, rainwater, and fresh air, and embraced the trees, plants, and bodies of water around it. Environmental architecture like this should be highlighted and implemented more in everyday structures from now until the foreseeable future to make a positive change on the environment. Learning how to protect the site that is being built on can help to conserve land, native plants, and wildlife; learning how to use locally and naturally sourced materials can reduce carbon emissions from transporting otherwise sourced materials and allows for the structure to age naturally with its environment. Projects like Casa Club Cardo Santo are important to study and learn from so that more effort can be made to create environmental architecture.
